About Me

I am a recently graduated PhD with both academic and industrial experiences. By combining my academic and industry profiles, I am able to translate complex technical problems into long-term objectives, realistic roadmaps, and rational solutions. As an engineer by education and experience, I am curious and pragmatic in my problem solving approach.

Work Experience

For the last five years, I worked as a Senior Data Scientist for Quantmetry in Paris, France. I participated in multiple data science projects, implementing real-world machine learning models with demonstrated impact. I worked as part of a team to maintain projects in production and familiarized myself with robust software engineering practices, such as continuous integration, containerization, APIs, tests, or documentation. For example, I contributed to pushing neural models in production for classifying, summarizing, and automating email replies in one of the largest French insurance companies. My role went beyond implementation since I contributed from ideation, experiments, framing, deployment, to monitoring. Furthermore, I ensured the various stakeholders' communication, commitment, and belief in the solution. Finally, I had the opportunity to coach junior data scientists in the team by translating business requirements into functional specifications.

Research Interests

My research focuses on analyzing the role of structure in building sentence embeddings. In that regard, I implemented innovative neural network architectures and published several articles in top peer-reviewed NLP conferences. My PhD, entitled Sentence embeddings and their relation with sentence structures, studies how neural networks compose text units to build sentence embeddings. In my research, I apply linguistic insights to neural network architectures. I design and implement dynamic architectures following tree or graph syntactic patterns. I aim to quantify the impact of linguistic bias on neural network architectures and how compositionality might be leveraged through the network structure. Along with linguistics, my work involves implementing complex structured neural networks as well as pre-training large language models at scale such as a version of GPT-2 for French with over a billion parameters.
